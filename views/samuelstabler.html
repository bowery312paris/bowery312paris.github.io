Samuel Stabler

Lives and works in Athens, Georgia

In early 2011 Stabler arrived at what is now the unmistakable signature of his early career: ink drawings of history’s hallowed paintings, offset by a near-obfuscating neon yellow palate. They are painstakingly detailed and to-scale, a feat underscored by their modest dimensions, typically one-tenth the size of their towering originals. More recently, Stabler has experimented with working on a larger scale and his imagery has broadened to include, most notably, the American West. He has also made a series of cutouts, which animate the tug between figure and scene like never before – his most abstract works, they are surprisingly good analogs to his drawings and present the viewer with a more challenging visual experience both topographic and straight-on, flat and full of depth.
